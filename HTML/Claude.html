Welcome to the Claude Monet Museum!

Explore the life and art of the renowned French painter, Claude Monet, at our museum. Immerse yourself in the beauty of his masterpieces and learn about the history behind each stroke of the brush. The exhibition is thoughtfully organized to provide a chronological journey through Monet's life, presenting key works from his early years to the later, iconic pieces that define his legacy. Each gallery reveals the artist's mastery of capturing the play of light, color, and atmosphere, whether it be in his tranquil landscapes, vibrant gardens, or the famous Water Lily series. The curation pays homage to Monet's dedication to exploring the nuances of nature and the ever-changing effects of light on his subjects.

BERTHE MORISOT AND THE ART OF THE 18TH CENTURY

WATTEAU, BOUCHER, FRAGONARD, PERRONNEAU

From 18 October 2023 to 3 March 2024, the Musée Marmottan Monet will present a very special exhibition, entitled Berthe Morisot and the Art of the 18th Century. The exhibition is curated by art historians Marianne Mathieu and Dominique d’Arnoult, with the participation of Claire Gooden, Head of Conservation at the Musée Marmottan Monet. Sixty-five art works from French and international museums, as well as private collections are brought together here for the first time to highlight the links between the work of the first female Impressionist Berthe Morisot (1841-1895) and the art of Antoine Watteau (1684-1721), François Boucher (1703-1770), Jean-Honoré Fragonard (1732-1806), and Jean-Baptiste Perronneau (1715-1783). Morisot's art, characterized by its subtle brushstrokes and keen observations of daily life, reflects the changing societal norms and roles of women during her era. The exhibition delves into the connections between Morisot's oeuvre and the artistic traditions of the 18th century, illuminating the influence and departure she embodied.

WATER LILY

PARIS, FRANCE

The Claude Monet Museum offers an enchanting exploration of the iconic Water Lily series, a masterpiece collection that embodies the essence of Claude Monet's artistic brilliance. As visitors enter the dedicated gallery, they are immediately immersed in a serene atmosphere created by the vibrant hues and delicate brushstrokes of Monet's water lilies. Each canvas tells a unique story, depicting the evolving seasons and moods of Monet's beloved Giverny garden. This exhibition not only celebrates Monet's genius but also serves as a visual journey, inviting art enthusiasts to step into the enchanting realm of water lilies and experience the timeless allure of Monet's vision.

MONET MASTERPIECES FROM THE MUSÉE MARMOTTAN MONET, PARIS

PARIS, FRANCE

As part of our international program, the Musée Marmottan Monet and Arthemisia are presenting a monographic exhibition at the CentroCentro, from September 21, 2023 to February 25, 2024, dedicated to the master of Impressionism, Claude Monet (1840-1926), entitled “Monet, masterpieces from the Musée Marmottan Monet, Paris”. ⁣⁣⁣ For the first time since its foundation in 1934, the Parisian Museum is showing a remarkable group of sixty major works from its collections in Spain. Among them, 44 paintings, drawings and sculptures will be presented to the Spanish public for the first time. ⁣⁣⁣ These works come exclusively from the Musée Marmottan Monet in Paris, universal legatee of the painter’s family and custodian of the artist’s first worldwide collection.

VAN GOGH EXPERIENCE, THE STARRY NIGHT

SAINT ANNA, WATTEAU, PERRONNEAU

The Van Gogh Experience, featuring the iconic masterpiece "The Starry Night," is a captivating immersion into the brilliant mind and artistic vision of Vincent van Gogh. As visitors step into the exhibition, they are enveloped in a multi-sensory journey that transcends traditional art appreciation. The centerpiece of the experience is "The Starry Night," a masterpiece that epitomizes van Gogh's unique blend of emotion, color, and texture. The exhibition design meticulously recreates the swirling night sky of "The Starry Night," allowing patrons to feel the dynamic movement of the celestial bodies as if they were stepping into the very canvas itself.
